Managing Debt

If it happens to be too late to contest your ticket in court, and now you are struggling with debt, there are still always real solutions.

Appealing Your Violations

Even once you are in debt with the City of Chicago from receiving several tickets, you still have the option of appealing your violations. This can remarkably reduce the amount of debt you have and put you in a better financial position.

Declaring Bankruptcy

If you have late penalties on your tickets that have caused you to have your vehicle impounded, declaring bankruptcy is a solution that can help you regain possession of your vehicle

Consider Using These Defenses If the Conditions Match Your Situation

Automated speed enforcement violations

  • The facts alleged in the violation notice are not consistent with a finding that Section 9-12-070, 9-12-075, or 9-12-077 was violated
  • Vehicle was an emergency vehicle
  • The vehicle's operator was issued a Uniform Traffic Citation for a speeding violation that occurred within 1/8th of a mile and 15 minutes of the violation that the system recorded
  • The facts provided in the ticket are not consistent with the necessary findings of a violation

Parking violation

  • Parking signs were missing or obscured
  • Parking meter was broken or inoperable
  • License plate and/or vehicle were stolen
  • At the time of the ticket, the vehicle's condition was legal

Red light violation

  • License place and/or vehicle were stolen
  • Vehicle was an emergency vehicle
  • Vehicle was participating in a legal funeral procession
  • The facts provided in the ticket are not consistent with the necessary findings of a violation
  • At the time of the ticket, you did not own the vehicle

Bus stop/lane violations

  • The vehicle was making a right hand turn
  • The non-taxicab vehicle was stopped to expeditiously drop off/pick up passengers and the vehicle did not impede the progress of any busses
  • The taxicab was stopped to expeditiously load or unload passengers
  • The facts are inconsistent with the necessary findings that Section 9-12-060(b) or Section 9-64-140(b) were violated
